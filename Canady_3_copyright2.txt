Copyright- The rules an original work has for beinng copied or used.
Copyleft- Similar to copyright, but requires that any modification follows the same rules as the original.
Fair Use- Using something correctly under its copyright rules.
Free Speech- The right under the first Amendment 
Free Software- Software that is generally free and is open to be chanegd, shared, used, etc.
Freeware- software that is free, i.e. not paid for
Public Domain- When something is open to be used by everyone, usually when the owner or creator is long-dead
File-sharing- Copying a file to someone else without them buying it, usually violates DRM or copyright
Piracy- Copying, ripping, torrenting, etc. something so others don't have to pay for it
Plagiarism- Copying some else's work (usually words) without giving credit
Infringement- Breaking the rules of copyright
Proprietary- Something that has to be paid for/ is owned by someone
Open Source- Can be freely used, changed, shared, similar to free software
GNU GPL License- A free software license that gaurantees users the rights to
use it freely
MIT License- A permissive free license, it permits reuse within
proprietary software (???)
Apache License- A free software license made by the FSF, still requires
that you keep the copyright notice/disclaimer
EULA- End-User License Agreement, the really long document that you check and
nobody ever reads
Creative Commons- a copyright license that lets people use, change, etc. your original work
